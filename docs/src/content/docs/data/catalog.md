---
title: The Catalog
description: How to browse and load datasets from Graticule's built-in geographic data library.
---

The catalog is Graticule's built-in library of geographic boundary data. It opens by default when you first load the app. Click **Catalog** in the top toolbar to open or close it at any time.

## Browsing datasets

Datasets are grouped by source and displayed with a name and region tag. Use the **search bar** at the top of the catalog to find a specific dataset by name, or use the filters to narrow things down.

### Filters

Two filters are available:

- **Type** — filters by the kind of geography: Regions (countries, states, counties), Cities & Places, Physical Geography (coastlines, rivers, lakes), Infrastructure (roads, railroads), or Utilities (time zones, reference grids).
- **Region** — filters to a specific part of the world: World, Africa, Asia, Europe, North America, South America, Oceania, or USA.

Filters combine — you can filter to, say, European administrative regions by setting both.

## Loading a dataset

Click any dataset to add it to your map as a new layer. The catalog closes and the data appears on the canvas. A loading indicator shows on the layer while a large dataset is downloading.

## Data sources

The catalog draws from several authoritative public sources. Each dataset is labelled with its source:

- **Natural Earth** — the most widely used source for world maps. Covers countries, states/provinces, coastlines, rivers, lakes, and more at multiple scales (10m, 50m, 110m). Public domain.
- **US Census TIGER** — authoritative US boundaries: states and counties. Highly detailed and regularly updated. Public domain.
- **Eurostat** — EU administrative and statistical regions at the NUTS 1, 2, and 3 hierarchy levels. CC BY 4.0.
- **Project Linework** — hand-curated and stylized linework collections, designed to look good on maps. Owned by the Graticule team.

See [Data Sources](/reference/data-sources) for more detail on each.

## Uploaded datasets

Files you've uploaded appear in a separate **Uploaded** section at the bottom of the catalog. You can re-add an uploaded dataset to the map without uploading it again — it persists in the catalog for the duration of your session.
